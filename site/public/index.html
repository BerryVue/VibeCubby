Why this exists

Apps can be home-cooked meals.

Not everything needs to be a startup. Sometimes you just want a grocery list that syncs with your partner's phone, or a chore wheel for the kids. AI made those apps minutes to build — and still hours to put somewhere. That's the part VibeCubby fixes.

🏡

Yours, actually.

Runs on your own Cloudflare account, free tier. Your data lives in your database. Export everything, always. Nobody can rug-pull your shelf.

🤖

Any agent.

Publish from Claude Code, Codex, Cursor — anything that can run a command. Your cubby doesn't care which AI you're loyal to this week.

👵

For your people.

Every app is a link your mom can open. Install to home screen like a real app. No accounts, no app store — just an optional PIN for the private stuff.

🎨

Make it YOURS.

Full theming, MySpace rules apply: your cubby should look like you. Good design is the flex here, not clever code.

The magic trick

"Publish this to my cubby."

Five words. Then it's live.

You just finished vibe-coding a chore wheel. Instead of letting it die on localhost like the last six ideas, you say the words. Your agent builds it, ships it to your Cloudflare account, and hands you a link that works on your phone, your partner's phone, and grandma's iPad.

npx vibecubby publish ./chore-wheel

you > claude > claude > ✓ live → jonathan.vibecubby.com/chore-wheel

Quickstart

Live in about 10 minutes. $0.

All you need is a free Cloudflare account and Node 18+. Your cubby deploys to a free workers.dev URL — point your own domain at it whenever you're ready.

Clone the cubby

Grab the open-source repo. It's AGPL licensed — free to make yours, forever, and nobody can take it away and close it up.

Run setup

One command logs into Cloudflare, creates your database, sets your PIN, and deploys the shelf.

Publish things

Tell your agent "publish this to my cubby" — or run the CLI yourself. Add it to your phone's home screen.

# one cubby, coming up
git clone https://github.com/BerryVue/VibeCubby my-cubby
cd my-cubby
npm install && npm run setup

"How is this different from Lovable / Val Town / GitHub Spark?"

Honest answer: those are excellent builders with hosting attached — and they host your apps on their platform, in their style, tied to their tool. VibeCubby is the opposite bet: you bring any agent, you own the infrastructure, and the shelf is an expression of you. If you want a managed platform, use theirs — genuinely. If you want a home, build a cubby.
